Air Drying — All Cat 1 & 2 (no dryer)
Jeans
Hang upside down clipped at the waistband — the thick reinforced band at the very top. Legs hang down. Dries the heaviest part first.
T-shirts
Hang upside down clipped at the hem — the stitched border at the very bottom of the shirt. Prevents shoulder bumps. Never hang from the neck hole — it stretches permanently.
Button shirts
On a proper hanger (not thin wire), right-side out. 1–2 middle buttons loosely done. Collar spread flat.
Track pants
Hang by the waistband, legs down. Or lay flat on rack. Synthetics dry quickly either way.
Shake each item firmly before hanging to knock out creases. Leave space between items — touching clothes dry slowly and smell musty. Keep darks out of direct strong sunlight.
Category 3
Cotton Innerwear
Underpants · Socks · Ganjis (undershirts) · Handkerchiefs  |  Regular wash  |  Tumble dried
⚠
10+ days = 2 washer loads. Drum fits roughly 8–10 underpants + 6–8 sock pairs + 4–5 ganjis per load. Measure detergent separately for each load.
Washer Settings
  • CycleNormal
  • TemperatureWarm
  • Spin SpeedHigh
  • Extra RinseON
  • TumbleFreshON — your default
  • Heavy SoilOFF
  • PrewashOFF
  • Save Time / Warm RinseOFF
  • Fabric SoftenerDO NOT USE
Warm is essential — kills bacteria, removes body oils. No softener ever: reduces absorbency and irritates skin. Never use on socks — makes them slippery.
Dryer Settings
  • CycleNormal
  • Dryness LevelDry (Normal)
  • Wrinkle ShieldON
  • GentleOFF
  • Steam / Save TimeOFF
  • Max fill level¾ full
Don't open door mid-cycle. Shake socks out before loading — they clump and insides won't dry when balled up.
Loading & Prep Tips
  • 1Socks: Turn right-side out before loading. Never put in balled up — inside won't wash.
  • 2Handkerchiefs: Unfold completely flat before loading.
  • 3Stains: Dab Tide directly on stain, rub gently, wait 5 min, then load. Far more effective than the wash cycle alone.
  • 4Lint screen: Clean before loading the dryer — every single load.
  • 5Bottom filter: Clean every 5 loads. Small door at bottom front of dryer.
  • 6Water tank: Empty when indicator light comes on. Pull out, empty into sink, slide back in.
Category 4
Towels
2 Egyptian cotton · 1–2 thin cotton · 2 hand towels  |  All washed together  |  Tumble dried
✕
Never mix towels with clothes — lint transfers permanently onto fabric. Never use fabric softener on any towel — coats fibres and destroys absorbency. Egyptian cotton is especially sensitive.
Washer Settings
  • CycleNormal
  • TemperatureWarm
  • Spin SpeedMedium
  • Extra RinseON
  • TumbleFreshON — your default
  • Heavy Soil / PrewashOFF
  • Save Time / Warm RinseOFF
  • Fabric SoftenerDO NOT USE — ever
Medium spin for Egyptian cotton — high spin stresses the long fibres and makes them rough over time.
Dryer Settings
  • CycleNormal
  • Dryness LevelDry (Normal)
  • Wrinkle ShieldON
  • Gentle / Steam / Save TimeOFF
  • Max fill level½ to ¾ full
Egyptian cotton: unload within 30–60 min even with Wrinkle Shield ON — extended tumbling after fully dry can roughen the long fibres.
After Drying — The Fluffing Step
  • 1Remove towels while still slightly warm — don't let them cool in the drum.
  • 2Give each towel a firm snap-shake in the air before folding. This loosens fibres and creates fluffiness — the single most important step.
  • 3Fold loosely — don't compress tightly on a shelf right after drying.
  • 4Egyptian cotton ID: noticeably heavier and denser than regular cotton. Terry loops (the small bumps on the surface) are longer and tighter. Absorbs water almost instantly on contact.
Category 5
Percale Bedsheets & Pillow Covers
1 Percale set (dark blue) + occasional extra cotton sheet  |  Always alone  |  Dryer to Damp Dry then straight to bed
!
Critical: Percale is the most delicate item you wash. Uses Delicates cycle on BOTH washer and dryer. Take out at Damp Dry — not bone dry — and go straight to bed. Never softener. Never mix with towels (lint) or items with zippers/buttons/hooks (snag damage).
Washer Settings
  • CycleDelicates
  • TemperatureCool
  • Spin SpeedMedium
  • Extra RinseON
  • TumbleFreshON — your default
  • All other optionsOFF
  • Fabric SoftenerDO NOT USE — ever
Cool = between Cold and Warm. Dark blue Percale specifically needs Cool. Not Warm (fades colour). Not Cold (won't remove skin oils). Detergent: 20 ml sheet set only, 25 ml if adding extra cotton sheet.
Dryer Settings
  • CycleDelicates
  • Dryness LevelDAMP DRY ← intentional
  • GentleON — reduces heat further
  • Wrinkle ShieldOFF — remove promptly
  • Steam / Save TimeOFF
  • Max fill level½ full maximum
Wrinkle Shield is OFF. Take sheets out at Damp Dry and go straight to the bed. Bone-dry Percale in the drum = deep set wrinkles that are very hard to remove.
Wrinkle Handling — The Correct Sequence
  • 1Remove at Damp Dry — sheets feel cool and barely damp to the touch, not wet.
  • 2Act immediately — take each sheet directly to the bed. Don't leave in a pile — Percale wrinkles set in minutes.
  • 3Fitted sheet: spread on bed, pull corners taut, smooth outward from centre with both hands. Finishes drying crisp on the bed.
  • 4Flat sheet: firm snap-shake in the air first, then fold while slightly damp. Damp folding = crisper, cleaner folds than folding dry.
  • 5Pillow covers: put straight onto pillows while damp, smooth by hand. Dry crisp and wrinkle-free on the pillow.
  • 6Too wrinkled? Put back in dryer with a damp cloth for 10 min on Delicates. Steam relaxes the fibres. Take straight to bed after.
  • 7Ironing: iron while still slightly damp on low heat only. Never iron bone-dry Percale.
